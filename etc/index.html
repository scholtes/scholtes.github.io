Youtube channels

These are some YouTube channels I like.

  • Two Minute Papers
    Károly Zsolnai-Fehér takes various research papers and condenses them down into videos that are generally under 5 minutes long. They are like annotated abstracts in video form. Topics mainly relate to computer science: graphics processing, machine learning, computational sciences, etc.

  • PBS Space Time
    In-depth videos on the concepts and mathematics of physics, astrophysics, and cosmosology.

  • Science & Futurism With Isaac Arthur
    High quality, episode-length videos (~30 minutes) that discuss topics in science and futurism, being imaginitive with what advanced civilizations could achieve.
    In particular, he likes to discuss these topics in the context of thermodynamics and compare them to works of science fiction (in particular, pointing out flaws in various works of fiction).

  • 3Blue1Brown
    Makes topics in higher mathematics more accessible and understandible to students and enthusiasts with high quality animations and by explaining the abstract thinking that mathematicians use to solve problems.

  • ElectroBOOM
    An electrical engineer who teaches basic concepts in electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism in a zany, wacky, comical style that often involves humorous self-harm.

  • Matthias Wandel
    Matthias is a Canadian carpenter and craftsman who solves many of his problems in his shop with some basic ingenuity. His videos are educational and show how he designs and executes his plans in shop.
